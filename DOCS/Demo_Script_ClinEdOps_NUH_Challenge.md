# ClinEdOps — Live Demo Talking Script  
**Aligned with:** NUH Medical Affairs (Education) — *Unified clinical education ops through an integrated digital platform* (Call 28, March 2026)

Use this script as a **guide**, not a rigid teleprompter. Pause for questions where marked. Adjust depth to your audience (executives vs. operations vs. IT).

---

## Before you go live (30 seconds, internal)

- Log in as **super_admin** or **programme_admin** for the “operations” arc; keep a **tutor** and **supervisor** account ready for quick role switches (or two browser profiles).
- Seed or prep data so **at least one posting**, **teaching session**, and **survey** path is visible—empty states kill momentum.
- Hide personal data; use demo emails and synthetic names.

---

## Opening hook (1–2 min)

**Say:**

> “NUH delivers clinical education across Medicine, Allied Health, Nursing, and training programmes. The brief puts it plainly: the **same core work**—onboarding, postings, tutor engagement, teaching hours, feedback, and reporting—has been happening in **parallel silos**: Excel, FormSG, different systems for medicine versus everyone else.  
> 
> That creates pain we can all feel: **duplicated effort**, **missed submissions**, **wrong student–tutor matching**, **reporting delays**, and **survey fatigue** when students get pinged after every micro-interaction.  
> 
> **ClinEdOps** is our answer to the challenge question: *How might we streamline and standardise fragmented workflows through a single system?*  
> 
> In the next [X] minutes I’ll show you **one platform**, **one source of truth**, and **role-based views** so Sarah in Allied Health ops, Dr Aisha on the ward, and Bryan in Medical Affairs leadership each get what they need—without reconciling CSVs on a Sunday night.”

**Optional audience question:**

> “Who here has ever merged two spreadsheets and prayed they were the same version?” *[light laugh, hands]*

---

## Set expectations (30 sec)

**Say:**

> “I’ll move in **user story order**: first how we **run the programme**—students, tutors, postings—then **teaching hours** and **approvals**, then **feedback and surveys**, then **reporting and governance**. Stop me anytime.”

---

## Flow 1 — One lifecycle, four disciplines (3–4 min)  
**Pain point addressed:** *Fragmented student lifecycle; no integrated pre-boarding → active → completed view.*

**Navigate:** Dashboard → **Students** / **Tutors** / **Postings** / **Departments** / **Academic cycles** (as your build exposes).

**Say:**

> “The brief asks for **centralised** student and tutor records across Medicine, Allied Health, Nursing, and training— with **clear status** for onboarding, active posting, and completion. Here, discipline isn’t an afterthought; it’s a **first-class filter**.  
> 
> Instead of one tracker per programme, we have **one directory** with the relationships the hospital actually uses: who is on which **posting**, which **department**, which **cycle**. That directly attacks **incorrect matching** and **inconsistent data standards**.”

**Demo beats:**

1. Open **Students** (or **Tutors**) — show list with discipline / status.  
   *“This is where ‘Sarah’ stops maintaining three Excel masters.”*

2. Open a **posting** or student detail — show link to tutors / department / cycle.  
   *“The posting is the hub—the brief’s requirement to assign students to departments, postings, tutors, and academic cycles.”*

**Transition:**

> “Once people and placements are real in the system, **teaching** and **feedback** can attach to the **right pairing**—not to a loose email thread.”

---

## Flow 2 — Teaching hours: tutor-first, structured (3–4 min)  
**Pain point addressed:** *Inaccurate hours; low compliance when only students log; cumbersome logging; limited visibility of approval status.*  
**Persona:** *Dr Aisha — fuss-free logging.*

**Navigate:** Switch to **tutor** (or stay admin and narrate tutor view) → **Teaching sessions** → **Log** / create session.

**Say:**

> “The challenge calls out a vicious cycle: hours are **manual**, sometimes **student-submitted**, so compliance is low and records drift. ClinEdOps standardises capture so **tutors log** structured sessions—scheduled, ad hoc, consultation—with **students and postings** tagged.  
> 
> Dr Aisha shouldn’t need a second job as data entry. If she can log a session in **minutes** and see it move through **approval**, we’ve removed the ‘email the medical education office’ detour.”

**Demo beats:**

1. **Create or open a teaching session** — show type, time, linkage to posting/students.  
2. **Submit for approval** (if applicable) — *“Status is visible; no black hole.”*  
3. Mention **billing hours** if in scope — *“Approved hours can align with billable constructs—the brief’s link to compensation processing.”*

**Transition:**

> “Logging is only half the trust story. Someone has to **say yes**—that’s governance, not bureaucracy.”

---

## Flow 3 — Approvals and discipline scope (2–3 min)  
**Pain point addressed:** *Data accuracy and governance; RBAC and approval hierarchies.*

**Navigate:** **Supervisor** or **programme admin** → teaching session **approve / reject** (or equivalent).

**Say:**

> “The brief is explicit: **role-based access** and **approval workflows**. Supervisors and programme admins see what they’re allowed to see—typically **their discipline**—and can **approve or reject** with audit-friendly outcomes.  
> 
> That’s how we move from ‘trust the spreadsheet’ to **defensible records** for appraisal and billing.”

**Demo beats:**

1. Show **approve/reject** on a pending session.  
2. One line on **discipline scoping** — *“A nursing programme admin isn’t editing medicine’s cohort by accident.”*

---

## Flow 4 — Surveys & feedback: templates, completion, fatigue (4–5 min)  
**Pain point addressed:** *Survey overload; inefficient monitoring; low scores and compulsory text; strategic visibility.*

**Navigate:** **Survey templates** → **Pending surveys** / student **fill** path → **Survey analytics** or tutor-facing feedback view.

**Say:**

> “Two problems in one paragraph in the brief: first, **survey fatigue** from firing a survey after every tiny touch; second, **no real-time visibility** of who’s done what, so admins chase people manually.  
> 
> ClinEdOps supports **configurable templates**—by discipline and context—with **structured scoring** and room for **mandatory comments when scores are low**, so red flags aren’t buried in averages. Pair that with **notifications and reminders** for pending work, and you’ve automated the nagging without losing the humanity.”

**Demo beats:**

1. Show a **template** — disciplines, question style, scoring out of 5 where applicable.  
2. Show **pending** vs **completed** — *“This is the compliance dashboard the brief asked for.”*  
3. If available, show **low-score** or escalation path — *“Reasons for low scores surface to the right roles.”*

**Transition:**

> “When feedback is structured at capture, **reporting stops being a weekend copy-paste project**.”

---

## Flow 5 — Reports, exports, and leadership oversight (3–4 min)  
**Pain point addressed:** *Reporting bottlenecks; manual consolidation; strategic oversight.*  
**Persona:** *Bryan — Medical Affairs / HOD-style oversight.*

**Navigate:** **Reports** → run or download; **Strategic insights** / analytics dashboard if available.

**Say:**

> “Bryan’s story in the brief is familiar: positive feedback for awards, negative feedback for improvement, everything **reformatted by hand**, disciplines **out of sync**.  
> 
> Here we’re aiming at the success metric: cut report generation from **minutes to under a minute** for the right cuts—by tutor, department, discipline, completion rates. **Export** supports finance, appraisal, and accreditation **without** re-keying.”

**Demo beats:**

1. Generate or open a **report** / export.  
2. Show a **dashboard** slice — hours + survey completion if visible.  
3. Name the outcome — *“One source of truth for ‘how are we doing?’—not five.”*

---

## Flow 6 — Notifications, broadcasts, and the “nudge engine” (2 min)  
**Pain point addressed:** *Notifications for pending and incomplete tasks; reducing manual follow-up.*

**Navigate:** **Notifications**; **Broadcast** (super admin) if applicable.

**Say:**

> “The brief wants **alerts** when work is pending. Notifications turn the platform from a passive database into a **workflow copilot**—students finish surveys, tutors submit hours, approvers see queues.”

---

## Flow 7 — Batch operations & imports (2 min)  
**Pain point addressed:** *Batch upload; duplicate detection; reducing Excel drudgery.*  
**Persona:** *Sarah — batch onboarding.*

**Navigate:** **Student** / **Tutor** **import** (batch upload).

**Say:**

> “Sarah’s world runs on **cohorts**. The brief asks for **batch upload** with **duplicate and error detection**. This is where we meet her halfway between ‘impossible perfect data’ and ‘ship the cohort Monday’—upload, validate, fix rows, commit.”

---

## Flow 8 — Governance: audit trail & admin console (2–3 min)  
**Pain point addressed:** *Audit trail; RBAC; security and PDPA-minded operations.*

**Navigate:** **Admin console** (super admin) — **audit logs**, **users**, **RBAC** as appropriate.

**Say:**

> “Hospitals don’t get to say ‘trust us’ without **evidence**. Full **audit trails** on key actions, **role-based permissions**, and secured access aren’t nice-to-haves—they’re in the brief. ClinEdOps is architected for **who did what, when**—supporting compliance conversations, not scrambling when someone asks.”

**Keep it high level** unless the audience is security-heavy.

---

## Flow 9 — Student experience (1–2 min)  
**Pain point addressed:** *Participation and clarity; reducing friction so completion rates can rise.*

**Navigate:** **Student** view — **My sessions**, **Pending surveys**.

**Say:**

> “The success metric mentions **~30% higher survey completion**. Students get a **simple lane**: what to complete, what’s coming up. Less confusion, fewer abandoned forms—that’s the lever.”

---

## Closing (1 min)

**Say:**

> “To tie it back to the challenge: **one unified system** for lifecycle, hours, feedback, and reporting; **standardised data** across disciplines; **governance** built in; and a path to **integrate** with hospital systems like Jobplan, BLUE, or TAS when we move from prototype to production.  
> 
> ClinEdOps doesn’t digitise one form—it **removes the seams** between them. I’m happy to go deeper on any flow or open the architecture.”

---

## Quick reference — Pain point → what to show

| Challenge pain point | Demo anchor |
|----------------------|-------------|
| Fragmented lifecycle / Excel | Students, tutors, postings, cycles, departments |
| Disconnected data | Single platform, linked records |
| Inaccurate teaching hours | Tutor session log, student tagging, approval |
| Feedback monitoring gaps | Templates, pending/completed, notifications |
| Reporting bottlenecks | Reports, exports, dashboards |
| Survey fatigue | Sensible templates + batching narrative (tie to product rules) |
| RBAC / approvals | Role switch, approve/reject, discipline scope |
| Audit / compliance | Audit logs, admin RBAC |
| Batch onboarding | Import flows |
| Strategic oversight | Analytics / strategic insights + reports |

> “Forms are a slice. The brief’s pain is **orchestration**—relationships between people, time, and quality data. That’s what we’re demoing.”

**“Integration?”**  
> “Prototype proves workflow and data model. **APIs and batch exchange** are the bridge to BLUE, TAS, and Jobplan—the brief’s explicit next phase.”
